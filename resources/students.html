Quick summary

The School has a zero-tolerance approach to the misuse of Generative AI. Do not upload photos of students or staff to AI tools. Do not create deepfakes or AI content that harms or discriminates against people. Use AI responsibly for schoolwork and remember that most AI platforms have age restrictions.

Do

What you should do

  • Think privacy first. Keep people's images and personal details out of AI tools.
  • Use AI to help your learning, not to do your work for you.
  • Tell a member of staff quickly if you see harmful or illegal AI content.
Don't

What you must not do

  • Upload photos of students or staff to any AI platform.
  • Create deepfakes, face-swaps, or AI images that target, demean or discriminate.
  • Submit AI-generated work as your own or use AI to cheat on assessments.

Policy mapping: Appendix 2 (GenAI misuse; no photo uploads) and the ICT Acceptable Use Agreement.

Non-negotiables

  • No uploading photos of students or staff to any GenAI platform or app. (Zero-tolerance.)
  • No deepfakes or altered portraits of real people (students, staff, or anyone connected to the School).
  • No harmful or discriminatory AI content (e.g., abusive, racist, sexist, homophobic, or otherwise offensive).
  • No academic dishonesty — don't submit AI-generated work as your own or use AI to cheat on assessments.
Safeguarding — serious lines you must know

"Sharing of nudes or semi-nudes (also called youth-produced sexual imagery) also includes images created by AI. Sharing may be a criminal offence. The School treats incidents as a safeguarding matter and will investigate thoroughly."

Policy mapping: Appendix 2; Appendix 4 (Sharing of nudes/semi-nudes, upskirting); ICT Acceptable Use Agreement.

Using AI for schoolwork

Be responsible and honest

  • AI should help your learning, not replace it. Use AI to understand concepts, get feedback on ideas, or help with research — but the thinking and work should be yours.
  • Always be transparent. If you use AI to help with an assignment, mention it. When in doubt, ask your teacher.
  • Don't cheat. Submitting AI-generated work as your own is academic dishonesty and will be treated seriously.
Important things to remember
  • Age restrictions: Most AI platforms require users to be 13+ or 18+. Check before you sign up.
  • At school: You can use Microsoft Copilot through your school account, which has appropriate safeguards.
  • Data sharing: Every prompt you send to an AI is sharing your personal data. Think twice before typing sensitive information.

Good practice examples

  • Asking AI to explain a difficult concept in simpler terms
  • Getting feedback on essay structure or arguments
  • Brainstorming ideas for a creative project
  • Practicing language skills through conversation
  • Asking for help understanding feedback from teachers

Remember: AI tools can make mistakes and may give biased or outdated information. Always verify important facts and think critically about AI responses.

Examples & myth-vs-fact

"It's only for fun" — uploading a friend's photo to an AI app

Not allowed. You must not upload photos of any student or staff to GenAI platforms. This is treated seriously.

Policy mapping: Appendix 2; ICT Acceptable Use Agreement.

Face-swaps / deepfakes of staff or students

Not allowed. This counts as harmful/discriminatory content and falls under zero-tolerance misuse of AI.

Policy mapping: Appendix 2.

AI-created nudes or semi-nudes of a real person

Strictly prohibited. "Sharing of nudes or semi-nudes… includes images created by AI." This is a safeguarding matter and will be investigated thoroughly. Do not create, request, forward, or store such content.

Policy mapping: Appendix 4.

AI meme that targets a protected group

Not allowed. Offensive or discriminatory content (including AI-generated) is prohibited and may lead to sanctions.

Policy mapping: Appendix 2.

You receive harmful AI content

Don't forward it. Tell a teacher or member of staff as soon as possible and keep any helpful details. The School will support you and investigate the matter thoroughly.

Policy mapping: Section 8; Appendix 4.

Using AI to write your entire essay

Not allowed. This is academic dishonesty. AI can help you brainstorm, understand topics, or check your work, but the essay should be your own writing and ideas.

Policy mapping: Academic integrity policies.

Asking AI to explain a difficult maths concept

Good practice. Using AI to help understand concepts you're struggling with is a great way to support your learning. Make sure you then practice the skills yourself.

This helps your learning rather than replacing it.

Sharing personal information in AI prompts

Think carefully. Remember that AI companies may store your conversations. Avoid sharing sensitive personal details, addresses, phone numbers, or information about others.

Use Microsoft Copilot at school for better data protection.

If something goes wrong

Tell a teacher or any member of staff straight away (Head of Year is fine too).
Don't share it on. Keep useful details (what, where, when). Bring your device if asked.
We will help. The School treats incidents seriously and will support you through the process.

Policy mapping: Section 8 (report promptly; handled under Behaviour/Safeguarding policies).

Sanctions & monitoring (AI-specific)

  • Behaviour Policy applies. Sanctions are proportionate; in the most serious cases, permanent exclusion is possible.
  • Academic sanctions for dishonest use of AI may include redoing assignments, grade penalties, or formal academic misconduct procedures.
  • Monitoring: School internet and email use are monitored. Even deleted items can be traced. The School owns its systems and may examine or delete files and withdraw access.

Policy mapping: Sections 6 & 9; Appendix 4; academic integrity policies; policy ownership/monitoring note.
