Welcome back

From this academic year, students in Year 9 and above will have access to Microsoft Copilot using their school account. Copilot is suitable for ages 13+ and has been set up for education use.

Why we've approved Copilot

  • School-managed: You sign in with your school Microsoft account, so use is covered by our policies and protections.
  • Privacy-aware: It's designed for education with stronger data protection than many public tools. Still, don't share personal or sensitive information.
  • Age-appropriate: Copilot is available for 13+. Many other Generative AI services set higher minimum ages (often 16+ or 18+).
In school

Microsoft Copilot is the approved AI tool on school devices and networks. Other Generative AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT) are not available in school.

Use Copilot to support your learning, not to replace it. The same Behaviour Policy and Academic Integrity rules apply when using AI.

Quick summary

AI can be a brilliant learning tool when used well. It can help you explore ideas, explain tricky topics and improve your work. This guide shows how to use AI responsibly, the red lines you must not cross, and what to do if something goes wrong.

Do

What you should do

  • Think privacy first. Never share photos of people or personal information.
  • Use Microsoft Copilot through school accounts when available.
  • Be honest about AI help in your schoolwork.
  • Use AI to help your learning, not replace it.
Don't

What you must not do

  • Upload photos of students or staff to any AI platform.
  • Create deepfakes or altered portraits of real people.
  • Create harmful or discriminatory content that offends or discriminates.
  • Submit AI-generated work as your own or use AI to cheat on assessments.

Non-negotiables

AI is powerful, but there are clear lines to protect privacy, safety and fairness. These rules apply wherever you use AI â€” at school, at home and on personal devices.

  • No uploading photos of students or staff to any GenAI platform or app. (Zero-tolerance.)
  • No deepfakes or altered portraits of real people (students, staff, or anyone connected to the School).
  • No harmful or discriminatory AI content (e.g., abusive, racist, sexist, homophobic, or otherwise offensive).
  • No academic dishonesty â€” don't submit AI-generated work as your own or use AI to cheat on assessments.

Using AI for schoolwork

Be responsible and honest

  • AI should help your learning, not replace it. Use AI to understand concepts, get feedback on ideas, or help with research â€” but the thinking and work should be yours.
  • Always be transparent. If you use AI to help with an assignment, mention it. Your teachers need to know so they can support your learning properly.
  • Follow your teacher's instructions. Some assignments may specifically allow or prohibit AI use. When in doubt, ask!

Recommended approach

  • Use Microsoft Copilot through your school account when available â€” it has stronger privacy protections for students.
  • Remember age restrictions: Most AI platforms require users to be 13+ or 18+. Check the terms of use.

Remember

Academic dishonesty includes submitting AI-generated work as your own. Use AI as a learning tool, not a shortcut to avoid doing the work yourself.
